Privacy Policy (PP) for Bridgit Social

Updated: January 24, 2026 (Version 2.0)

The purpose of this Privacy Policy is to inform you about how Bridgit Social uses your personal data. We recommend that you read it carefully.

Key Definitions

For the purposes of this document, the following terms shall be defined as follows:

Application: Refers to the mobile application known as Bridgit Social, available for download on the Apple App Store.

Bridgit Group LLC: The legal entity under which Bridgit Social operates, incorporated in the State of New Jersey, United States.

Bridgit: Also referred to as "Bridge It!", the term describes the purpose of the platform as a digital bridge facilitating spontaneous, goal-driven connections between Users, and bridging the gap between online engagement and in-person interaction.

Terms (TOU): Refers to these Terms of Use governing the use of the Application and related Services.

User: Any individual who has registered for an Account and utilizes any features or Services offered through the Application.

User Details: Information voluntarily provided by a User, including but not limited to interests, professional background, networking intentions, and preferences. Certain details, such as age and residential area, may be optionally concealed at the User's discretion.

Networking Intentions: User-selected goals across four categories: Professional Networking, Dating, Socializing, and Seeking Solutions. These intentions help connect users with like-minded individuals.

Geographical Position: The User's location, which is recorded and regularly updated when the geolocation feature is enabled. A User's geographical position is not shared with other Users unless the "Start Networking" feature is activated.

Start Networking: The initiation of a User's networking experience within the Application. Upon activation, the User becomes visible to and can engage with other Users based on their availability and visibility settings, as defined during registration or as updated by the User.

Same Place Option: A feature that designates when two Users are located in the exact same physical place. This is recorded if the User enables geolocation and selects the specific place within the Application.

Proximity/Search Within Walking Distance Option: A feature that identifies Users who are geographically close to one another—within walking distance—but not necessarily located at the exact same place.

Walking Distance: Defined as the maximum distance between two Users such that an in-person meeting can occur within approximately 10 minutes of walking. This distance is calculated as up to one mile in both densely populated and less dense areas.

Map: A feature that displays other Users located within the same physical area or within walking distance, once the "Start Networking" feature is enabled. The Map also presents general anonymized statistics regarding nearby Users.

Mark to Meet: An action taken by a User to express interest in meeting another User. An optional note may be attached, specifying the meeting's purpose, proposed location, or any initial message.

Connection: An interaction that results from mutual interest between two Users, allowing them to communicate within the Application.

Feed: A feature allowing Users to share pitches and discover what others are looking for in their area. Feed posts are public and include approximate location for distance-based filtering.

AI Pitch Assistant: An AI-powered feature that helps Users create personalized networking pitches through conversational interaction.

My Growth: A section featuring referral programs, daily insights, event recommendations, and networking challenges to help Users grow their network.

Referral Program: A rewards program allowing Users to earn Premium subscription time by inviting friends to join Bridgit Social.

Services: The collective suite of features and functionalities—both complimentary and paid—offered by Bridgit Social to facilitate in-person, real-time personal and professional connections.

Premium Membership: A subscription-based service granting Users access to advanced features and enhanced functionality within the Application.

Standard Membership: The free tier of service providing core networking features with certain limitations.

Account: A registered User's individual profile granting access to the Application and its Services. Each User is permitted to maintain only one active Account at any given time.

3.1.2 Networking Intentions Data

Bridgit Social allows you to set your networking goals and intentions to help connect you with like-minded individuals.

Intention Categories and Selection

You may select one or more networking intentions across four categories:

  • Professional Networking (public): Find mentors, career opportunities, industry connections, business partnerships, knowledge sharing, and startup networking
  • Dating (public, requires explicit consent): Serious relationships or casual dating with people nearby
  • Socializing (public): Make new friends, find activity partners, join community events, expand your social circle, and connect through hobby groups
  • Seeking Solutions (public): Find expert advice, collaborate on projects, get recommendations, problem-solve, and learn new skills

You may also designate one intention as your primary intention (public), which will be highlighted on your profile.

IMPORTANT - Dating Category Special Consent

The Dating category is considered a sensitive category under GDPR Article 9 (special categories of personal data). By selecting the Dating category, you provide explicit consent for Bridgit Social to process this sensitive personal data for the purpose of connecting you with other users who have also selected dating intentions.

You may withdraw your consent for the Dating category at any time by:

  • Deselecting the Dating category in your profile settings
  • Contacting us at privacy@bridgitsocial.com

Withdrawing consent will remove the Dating category from your profile and stop processing this data, but will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

Visibility

Your selected intentions are PUBLIC and visible to all users who view your profile. Your primary intention is highlighted prominently. This helps other users understand your networking goals and facilitates relevant connections.

How We Use Intentions

  • Matching: We use your intentions to suggest relevant connections with users who share similar goals
  • Filtering: Users can filter their discovery feed based on intentions to find relevant connections
  • Personalization: We use intentions to personalize your feed, event recommendations, and AI pitch suggestions

Legal Basis:

  • Professional Networking, Socializing, Seeking Solutions: Execution of contract (providing matching service)
  • Dating: Explicit consent (GDPR Article 9)

3.1.3 AI Pitch Assistant Data

When you use the AI Pitch Assistant feature, we collect and process the following data:

  • Conversation with AI (session-only, not stored long-term): Your messages and questions to the AI assistant during the session
  • Profile Context Sent to AI (temporary): Limited profile information sent to generate personalized suggestions, including:
    • Occupation
    • Primary intention and other selected intentions
    • Interests (if provided)
    • General location (city, not precise coordinates)
  • Generated Pitch Suggestions (stored if you save them): AI-generated pitch suggestions provided to you
  • Selected and Customized Pitches (stored): Pitches you choose to save and any customizations you make

Third-Party AI Processing

We use OpenAI's API (or similar AI service providers) to generate personalized pitch suggestions. When you use the AI Pitch Assistant:

  • Limited profile data is sent to OpenAI for processing
  • Data sent includes: occupation, intentions, interests, and general location
  • Data NOT sent: full name, email address, precise location, or private messages
  • OpenAI processes this data to generate suggestions but does not store it beyond the processing session
  • We have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with OpenAI to ensure GDPR Article 28 compliance

Conversation Storage

AI conversations are session-only and are not stored in our database after your session ends. This means:

  • Your conversation history is deleted when you close the AI Pitch Assistant
  • We do not retain a record of what you discussed with the AI
  • Only the pitches you explicitly save are stored in your profile

Legal Basis: Consent. By using the AI Pitch Assistant, you consent to the processing of your profile data by OpenAI for the purpose of generating pitch suggestions. You may opt out by simply not using the AI Pitch Assistant feature.

3.1.4 Feed System Data

When you use the Feed feature to share and discover pitches, we collect:

  • Feed Posts (public): Your pitch content, timestamp, and approximate location
  • Approximate Location (public, fuzzy): Your general location at the time of posting, used for distance-based filtering. Location is shown to other users as distance (e.g., "2 miles away"), not exact coordinates. Location data is rounded to approximately 0.1-mile precision to protect your privacy
  • Interactions (private): Your likes, comments, and saves on feed posts
  • Viewing History (private): Which posts you viewed, used to improve recommendations

Third-Party Search Indexing

We use Algolia (a third-party search service) to power feed search and filtering. Feed posts are indexed in Algolia's servers for fast, relevant search results.

Data shared with Algolia includes:

  • Feed post content (pitches)
  • Approximate location (fuzzy, not precise)
  • Intentions
  • Timestamp
  • User ID (anonymized identifier)

Data NOT shared with Algolia: Full name, email, precise location, or private messages.

We have a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with Algolia to ensure GDPR Article 28 compliance and data security.

User-Generated Content

You are responsible for the content you post to the Feed. All feed posts must comply with our Terms of Use. We moderate content to ensure compliance and may remove posts that violate our policies.

Legal Basis:

  • Execution of contract: Providing feed functionality
  • Legitimate interest: Improving search and recommendations

Legal Basis:
